---

### Self-Referral vs. Self-Pay

**Self-referral** = scheduling a non-contrast MRI **without a provider's order**. Only non-contrast MRIs qualify.

**Self-pay** = how you **pay** for your scan — fixed, upfront pricing instead of billing insurance.

These are separate concepts:
- You can self-refer **and** self-pay (no order needed, pay out of pocket).
- You can self-pay **with** a provider's order (provider sent the order, but you choose not to use insurance).
- You **cannot** self-refer for contrast MRI, CT, X-Ray, Ultrasound, DEXA, or Arthrograms — all require a provider order.

## MRI Questions

---

### What Is an MRI?

MRI (magnetic resonance imaging) uses a powerful magnet and radio waves — **no X-rays or radiation** — to create detailed images of the inside of the body. It is non-invasive and painless.

**Best used for:** Soft tissues — muscles, tendons, ligaments, nerves, organs — that don't show well on X-ray.

---

### Common Reasons for MRI

**Spine & Nerves:** Back/neck pain, herniated or bulging discs, sciatica, pinched nerves, spinal stenosis, nerve compression.

**Joints & Muscles:** Knee/shoulder/hip pain, torn ligaments or tendons (ACL, rotator cuff), sports injuries, arthritis, joint inflammation.

**Brain & Head:** Chronic headaches/migraines, dizziness, balance problems, memory or vision changes, tumor/stroke/MS evaluation.

**Abdomen & Pelvis:** Liver, kidney, or pancreas concerns, uterus/ovaries/prostate evaluation, unexplained abdominal or pelvic pain.

**Other:** Foot/ankle pain, wrist/hand/elbow injuries, soft tissue masses or swelling, vascular evaluation.

---

### MRI Safety & Implants

MRI is safe for most people. Because the magnet is strong, every patient completes a safety screening.

**If the caller has an implant or device** (pacemaker, spinal stimulator, or any implanted hardware):
- They must call us **before** the appointment.
- Our technologists review safety information and confirm MRI compatibility.
- Patient should bring device paperwork, ID cards, or remotes.
- The scan **cannot be performed** until device information is verified.
- If we cannot confirm the implant is MRI-safe, the exam must be rescheduled.

**MRI is not recommended (or requires provider/tech clearance) if the patient has:**
- Certain pacemakers, cochlear implants, some aneurysm clips, or older neurostimulators not verified as MRI-safe
- Metal fragments or shrapnel — especially near eyes or brain
- Recent surgery with unverified metal implants or stents
- External/internal devices: insulin pumps, medication patches with metallic backing, external fixators
- Pregnancy in the first trimester (unless medically necessary and physician-approved)

---

### Claustrophobia & Anxiety

- Most scanners are **wide-bore MRI** — more open and spacious than traditional designs.
- With **Swift Scan AI**, most non-contrast exams finish in about **25 minutes or less**.
- **St. George offers Open MRI** — the most open experience, accommodates up to 650 lbs. Great for claustrophobia, anxiety, mobility challenges, or larger body types.
- We do **not offer sedation**. The patient should speak with their provider about anxiety medication if needed. If medication is taken, **a driver is required**.

---

### Contrast MRI

- The **provider decides** if contrast is needed — not the patient.
- Contrast MRIs use an injection to highlight certain tissues. Safe for most patients.
- **Contrast MRI always requires a provider order** — not eligible for self-referral.
- Our technologists walk the patient through the contrast process step by step.

---

### MRI Duration

- Most non-contrast scans: **under 25 minutes** (Swift Scan AI).
- Contrast or complex studies: **40–60 minutes**.
- Plan extra time for check-in and safety screening.

---

### MRI Experience

- The patient won't feel the imaging itself.
- The machine makes **loud tapping/knocking sounds** — ear protection and music are provided.

---

### MRI Preparation

## CT Questions

---

### What Is a CT Scan?

CT (computed tomography) uses X-rays and a computer to create detailed cross-sectional images. It is faster than most imaging and excellent for bones, lungs, abdomen, and emergency evaluations.

---

### MRI vs. CT

| | MRI | CT |
|---|---|---|
| **Technology** | Magnet + radio waves (no radiation) | X-rays (small radiation dose) |
| **Best for** | Soft tissues — brain, nerves, muscles, tendons, ligaments | Bones, lungs, abdomen, emergent scans |
| **Speed** | 25–60 minutes | 5–10 minutes |

The provider chooses the right test based on the patient's situation.

---

### CT Orders & Eligibility

**All CT scans require a provider order.** CT uses X-ray radiation, so a provider must confirm the test is medically appropriate and that benefits outweigh exposure. CT is **not eligible for self-referral**.

---

### CT Pricing & Insurance

We accept most insurance plans and offer transparent self-pay pricing. Costs are shared upfront with no surprises.

---

### CT Safety & Radiation

- CT uses a small amount of ionizing radiation.
- Our scanners use **dose-reduction technology and low-dose protocols**.
- Technologists follow **ALARA principles** (As Low As Reasonably Achievable).

**Pregnancy:** We do **not** perform CT on patients who are pregnant or may be pregnant. Discuss non-radiation alternatives with your provider.

---

### CT Contrast

- Some CT exams use **iodine-based contrast dye** to highlight organs and blood vessels.
- Most patients tolerate it well.
- **Tell us if the patient has:** prior contrast reaction, asthma, kidney disease, or is pregnant.
- If contrast is ordered, our technologist performs a safety check and walks the patient through the process.

---

### CT Preparation

- **Non-contrast CT:** No special prep needed for most exams.
- **Contrast CT:** May need to fast for 2–4 hours and arrive early.
- Wear comfortable, metal-free clothing. Bring medication list.
- Specific instructions provided at scheduling.

---

### CT & Claustrophobia

CT scanners are **short and open at both ends** — shaped like a donut. Most scans take only a few minutes, and the patient can see out the entire time. Let us know about anxiety and we'll talk them through it.

---

### CT Duration

- Most CT exams: **5–10 minutes**.
- With contrast: plan **30–45 minutes** including setup.

---

### CT Results

- Board-certified radiologist interprets images and sends report to the provider.
- Results typically available within **24–48 hours**, often sooner.
- We can provide: QR code, disc, or online patient portal access.
