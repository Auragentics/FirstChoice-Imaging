### MRI Safety & Eligibility

**Not eligible for MRI if you have:**
- Pacemaker
- Cochlear implant
- Aneurysm clips

**Spinal cord stimulator:** Must verify MRI compatibility before scheduling — patient must provide **make, model, and serial number**.

**Metal from prior surgery:** Typically safe — notify staff of any implants or metal in/on body. Notify staff of any prior **brain or heart surgery**.

**Claustrophobia / anxiety medication:** Contact your provider before the appointment. If medication is taken, **a driver is required**.

### Contrast vs. Non-Contrast Guide

**Non-contrast** — detailed imaging without dye. Good for: bones, muscles, tendons, joints, soft tissues, certain organs. Helps diagnose: fractures, herniated/bulging discs, cysts, infections, kidney stones, some tumors.

**Contrast** — medical dye enhances specific structures. Highlights: blood vessels, inflammation, abnormal growth, organ function, tumors/lesions. Administered via: IV (most MRI/CT), orally (select CT abdomen/pelvis), or injected into a joint (arthrograms).

**Who decides?** The referring provider determines if contrast is needed. Patients do not choose.

### Self-Referral MRI

A self-referral MRI lets patients schedule their own **non-contrast MRI** without a doctor's order or insurance approval. It saves time by skipping extra office visits and insurance authorization — patients can get scanned sooner and receive results faster.

**Eligible exams:** Non-contrast MRI only. Standard non-contrast MRI is **$299** — price is shared upfront at scheduling.

**Not eligible for self-referral (provider order required):**
- Any MRI requiring contrast
- CT, X-Ray, Ultrasound, DEXA, Arthrograms

**What's included:** MRI exam, board-certified radiologist report, image access — no hidden fees or surprise bills.

**Insurance & self-referral:**
- Self-referral uses our **self-pay program** — insurance cannot be billed because insurance companies require a provider order.
- Patients with insurance may still self-refer, but insurance will not cover the exam.
- To use insurance, the patient needs a provider order before scheduling.

**Doctor involvement:** Self-referral removes the extra step of scheduling an office visit just to get an MRI order. Patients are always encouraged to consult their provider with questions before or after the scan.

### Prior Authorization

A prior authorization is a process where your insurance company reviews medical documentation from your provider to determine if an imaging exam will be covered.

**May require prior auth:** MRI, CT exams (varies by plan).
**Typically does NOT require prior auth:** Ultrasound, X-ray, DEXA/bone density, self-pay exams.

**Processing time:** ~5–7 business days once documentation is received. Medicaid: up to 14 business days.

**Swift Scheduling:** Patients do **not** need to wait for authorization before scheduling. We schedule the appointment as soon as we receive the provider's order and process authorization in the background.

**If authorization is denied:** We notify the ordering provider. The provider may appeal, add documentation, or request expedited review. Patients may also contact their insurance directly.

**Common causes of delay:** Missing clinical notes, order type mismatch, missing diagnosis codes. Accurate documentation from the provider prevents delays.

### Insurance & Self-Pay

**Insurance patients:**
- Coverage and out-of-pocket cost vary by plan.
- Some plans require prior authorization.
- We verify benefits and estimate costs.
- Patient may owe deductible, copay, or coinsurance.

**Self-pay patients:**
- Fixed, transparent pricing.
- No prior authorization needed — schedule immediately.
- Self-referral available for non-contrast MRI exams.
- Does **not** apply to insurance deductible.
- Great for: high deductibles, no insurance, insurance denial, or wanting to bypass prior auth.

Patients may choose whichever option works best for their needs. For orders from a provider, patients may use self-pay or insurance.
