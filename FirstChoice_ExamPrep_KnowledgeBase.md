# First Choice Imaging — Exam Preparation Knowledge Base (Voice Agent)

---

## MRI Preparation

---

### What to Wear

- Wear soft, comfortable clothing **without metal** — sweatpants and a t-shirt are ideal.
- If clothing has metal snaps, zippers, or buttons, we provide **scrubs** to change into.
- Leave metal items at home: jewelry, watches, belts, hair clips.

---

### Implants & Medical Devices

**If the caller has any implant or device** (pacemaker, spinal stimulator, or other implanted hardware):
- Must **call us before the appointment** — do not wait until day of.
- Our technologists review device safety info and confirm MRI compatibility.
- Patient should bring: device paperwork, ID cards, remotes.
- **Scan cannot be performed** until device info is verified.
- If we cannot confirm MRI safety, the exam must be **rescheduled**.

---

### Claustrophobia & Anxiety

- Let us know **ahead of time** so we can prepare.
- Most scanners are **wide-bore MRI** — more open and spacious than traditional designs.
- **Swift Scan AI** technology — most non-contrast exams done in about **25 minutes or less**.
- **St. George offers Open MRI** — the most open experience, accommodates up to **650 lbs**. Good for claustrophobia, anxiety, mobility challenges, or larger body types.
- We do **not offer sedation**. Patient should speak with their provider about anxiety medication if needed.
- If medication is taken before the scan, **a driver is required**.

---

### Contrast MRI

- Some MRIs use a small amount of contrast dye via IV to highlight certain tissues or pathology.
- **Requires a provider's order** — not eligible for self-referral.
- Our technologist reviews the order before scheduling.
- The team walks the patient through the contrast process step by step.

---

### During the MRI Exam

- Technologist is present the entire time to explain what's happening.
- Patient receives **earplugs or headphones** for noise protection.
- Communication via **built-in speaker and microphone** throughout the scan.
- The scan is **painless** — patient just needs to lie still for the clearest images.

---

### After the MRI Exam

- Images reviewed by a **board-certified radiologist**.
- Results typically ready within **1–2 business days**, sent directly to the provider.
- Patient can also access report and images via:
  - Secure **patient portal**
  - Pick up at the office
  - Request by **email, QR code, or disc**

---

## CT Preparation

---

### What to Wear

- Wear comfortable, **metal-free clothing** if possible — otherwise we provide scrubs.
- Remove jewelry, glasses, or anything metal before the scan.

---

### Eating & Drinking

- **Most CT exams:** Eat and drink normally.
- If special prep is needed, instructions are provided at scheduling.

---

### CT Contrast Dye

- If the provider ordered CT with contrast, dye is given through a **quick IV injection**.
- Contrast highlights organs, blood vessels, and tissues for clearer images.
- **Hydrate well** before the appointment if contrast is planned.
- Our team reviews all details at scheduling so the patient knows what to expect.

---

### Claustrophobia & CT

- CT scanners are **open on both ends** — shaped like a donut, not a tunnel.
- Most scans take only **a few minutes**.
- The scan is painless. Technologist stays in contact the entire time.

---

### During the CT Exam

- The scan is **completely painless**.
- If contrast is used, the patient may feel a **brief warm sensation or metallic taste** during injection — this is normal and fades quickly.

---

### After the CT Exam

- Images reviewed by a **board-certified radiologist**.
- Results typically ready within **1–2 business days**, sent directly to the provider.
- Patient can also access report and images via:
  - Secure **patient portal**
  - Pick up at the office
  - Request by **email, QR code, or disc**
- Any post-scan instructions are given by the technologist at the appointment.
